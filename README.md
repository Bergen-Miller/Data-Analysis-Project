### Topic

We examine relationships between cross-county migration, household incomes, county gdp, and effective income taxes in the U.S. from 2011 to 2022.

### Motivation



### Research Questions

1. (Descriptive) Is there a relationship between household AGI and cross-county mobility?
2. (Causal) How do tax shocks influence cross-counrty migration among people of varying economic and spatial backrounds?
3. (Causal) What is the effect of cross-county migration on county GDP, and through what mechanisms (simply higher population, selection of types of people (labor productivity measured by income), changing tax-base)? 

Questions are ranked in order of completion (probaby).

### Data

The RData files are descriptively named, with incomes and taxes coming from IRS Statistics of Income (SOI), migration data coming from a different SOI database, and GDP data coming from the Bureau of Economic Analysis.

Incomes and GDP are mostly cleaned (which Berg did for his ECNS 432 project). Immigration data have not been cleaned at all and are specific to this project.

All data are county-by-year from 2011 to 2022.
